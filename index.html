👩‍🏫 About Ms. Karabetsos

My Life & Education

Welcome! I am Ms. Karabetsos, a special education teacher serving students in a self-contained kindergarten through 2nd grade autism classroom. I believe every student deserves meaningful access to learning, communication, and independence-building experiences.

My educational background and classroom practice are rooted in student-centered instruction, collaboration, family partnership, differentiated supports, and high expectations with compassionate teaching.

Professional Philosophy

  • Relationships come first.
  • Students learn best when barriers are reduced.
  • Communication, regulation, life skills, and academics all matter.
  • Families are essential members of the team.
  • Skills should transfer across school, home, and community environments.

How This Builds Family & Community Relationships

This section helps families understand who I am as an educator, what I value, and how I support children. That transparency builds trust and creates a shared foundation for collaboration.

🧩 Classroom Environment

📅 Predictable Routines

Students benefit from structure, consistency, and clearly sequenced daily expectations.

🖼️ Visual Supports

Visual schedules, task strips, and first-then boards support independence and understanding.

💬 Communication Supports

Students may use speech, AAC, gestures, visuals, and modeled language throughout the day.

🧠 Sensory & Regulation

Movement, calm spaces, and regulation supports help students participate more successfully.

Connection to Families and the Community

Families can use many of the same visuals, routines, and regulation tools at home and in community settings. This helps students generalize skills and increases consistency across environments.

📚 Current Content We Are Learning

Unique Learning System

Students are working on vocabulary, comprehension, communication, shared reading, and thematic learning tasks.

How parents can help at home:

  • Read short books together.
  • Name pictures and describe what is happening.
  • Ask simple WH questions.
  • Use repetition, visuals, and choices.

Eureka Math

Students are practicing counting, sorting, comparing, matching, and simple hands-on problem solving.

How parents can help at home:

  • Count toys, snacks, and household objects.
  • Sort items by color, size, or category.
  • Practice more, less, same, and one-to-one matching.
  • Use everyday routines for math talk.
